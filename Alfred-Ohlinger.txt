About this Super Smash Bros. Melee Database:
For a full overview of each entity and relation, please see the document I handed in with Phase 1.  The following are some additional notes to clarify a couple
changes I have made, some documentation of my data selection methods, and some tips to help the grader understand exactly what’s going on.

- The players I chose to put in my database were determined as follows:  First, I put in the top 100 players for each year from 2013-2016.  As there were
duplicates from year to year, this amounted to around 150 players.  Additionally, for each tournament I added to the database, I also added any player who got
top 64, top 48, or top 32 depending on the size of the tournament.  From these two methods, I ended up with my final total of 281 players.  These range from the
very best in the game to someone who happened to get 49th at a single tournament that I included in my database.

- I included 20 tournaments from within the last 18 months.  I tried to take at least one tournament from each region to get a wider sample of players.  Larger
regions not included were:  Europe, Mexico, and Japan.  This was because almost every player included in the top 64/48/32 for a foreign tournament would be a
new entry into my database, and data for these foreign players was much more difficult to find and record accurately.  I did include the 8 tournaments that were
the main criteria for determining the top 100 in 2016, as well as 7 other larger tournaments spanning regions, and 5 invitationals.  The invitationals had only
16 or 20 entrants and included only the very best players.  Each other tournament is open for anyone to enter.

- Because I chose only tournaments from the last 18 months (easier to find and more reliable data), but included people from the 2013 top 100 in my player
database, there were several players who did not end up with a tournament that they participated in.  Because of this, Player no longer totally participates
in the relation Attends.  These players are as follows: Darc, Jaden, Hanky Panky, Remen, PikaChad, Eggm, Lucien, Lord HDL, Trail, Hyprid, RaynEX, Dart!,
Jeapie, Reaper, OkamiBW, ZeRo, Larry Lurr, DaShizWiz, Vwins, Jman, Gucci, Blunted_Object10, Bam, Strawhat Dahean, Sion, Bob$ , Vist, and Lampadaires.  Only 28
out of the 281 total players didn’t have an associated tournament, which I am satisfied with for the current purposes of this database.

- I also went back through the top 100 players for each year and added any placings below 49th (top 64) to any tournaments they attended (Ex. 65th, 97th, etc.)
that are included in my database.   I did not do this for any players outside the top 100, so they may have attended a tournament in my database, but their
place isn’t recorded here if it was too low.  I simply didn’t have the time to add the attendance for each of these, but almost 1000 tuples are included in
Attends despite this.  For the purposes of this database, I am satisfied with the amount of relevant data I’ve included.

- Not every player has a real name and a city they live in.  As this database is based off real life people, I used many different sources to try to find
accurate information for each player, including the ssbwiki, tournament websites, YouTube videos of actual matches, and player social media pages.  However,
despite my best efforts, I wasn’t able to find everybody’s real name or specific city.  This differs from what I originally turned in during Phase 1, as
Player_Name and City in Player now have NULL values occasionally.

- Any teams included were added as I added players who were part of a team, so every team is still associated with at least one player.  I ended up with 68
teams with 97 players on those teams.

- All 26 characters in the game were added to Character, but not every character has a player who mains them, as I didn’t base adding players on their mains
(Ex. Pichu, Donkey Kong).  Each player has at least one main but no more than three (Changed from four in Phase 1).

- When I turned in phase 1, I didn’t fully understand how to properly use foreign keys in phpmyadmin.  I now do and any problems with my initial table creations
have been fixed so I can store my data properly (Changing the engine, properly adding foreign key references).

- I had to rename the character entity to game_character as character is a SQL keyword and caused problems in my queries.  Any previous references to character
refer to game_character.

- The grader will notice that I didn’t follow exactly every specification in my original plans.  I purposely had to change some things to keep this accurate to
the real world and what information I could find.  Hopefully, I will not get docked for these changes as they are in the spirit of providing only accurate
information.  Additionally, my core design is still intact.  As I worked on this project, I learned a lot about how to use the phpmyadmin’s tools better, so if
I were to start another project, I would have a better idea of how exactly to design everything from the beginning.
